Black holes are some of the most extreme objects in the universe—regions of space where gravity is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape. 

In recent years (and as recently as this week in **April 2026**), our understanding of these "cosmic vacuum cleaners" has shifted from theoretical mysteries to objects we can actually see and hear.

---

## Anatomy of a Black Hole
Think of a black hole not as a "hole," but as an incredibly massive amount of matter packed into a tiny area.



* **The Singularity:** The very center where matter is crushed into a point of infinite density. Here, our current laws of physics (General Relativity) break down.
* **The Event Horizon:** The "point of no return." Once something crosses this boundary, the escape velocity required exceeds the speed of light ($c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$), making exit impossible.
* **Accretion Disk:** A swirling "whirlpool" of gas and dust spinning around the black hole. Friction makes this disk so hot that it glows brightly in X-rays, which is how we "see" them.

---

## The Four Main Types
Black holes aren't "one size fits all." They are categorized by their mass:

| Type | Mass (Sun = 1) | Origin |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primordial** | Atomic to Mountain-sized | Formed just after the Big Bang. |
| **Stellar-Mass** | $5$ to $100$ Suns | Created when a massive star collapses. |
| **Intermediate** | $100$ to $100,000$ Suns | Likely formed from clusters of stars/black holes merging. |
| **Supermassive** | Millions to Billions | Found at the center of almost every galaxy (like our own Sgr A*). |

## 2025–2026 Breaking News
We are currently in a "Golden Age" of black hole discovery. Here are the most recent breakthroughs:

* **The "Space Tornadoes" (March 2025):** Astronomers using the ALMA telescope discovered massive "tornadoes" of gas swirling around **Sagittarius A*** (the black hole at the center of the Milky Way). This proved that even "quiet" black holes are much more active than we thought.
* **Primordial Black Hole Candidate (November 2025):** A strange gravitational wave signal (**S251112cm**) was detected from an object *lighter* than our Sun. Since stars can't form black holes that small, scientists believe this might be the first confirmed "Primordial" black hole from the birth of the universe.
* **The Markarian 501 Binary (April 2026):** Just days ago, researchers released images of two supermassive black holes orbiting each other so closely they are expected to **merge in as little as 100 years**—a blink of an eye in cosmic time.

## What happens if you fall in?
If you were to fall into a stellar-mass black hole, you would experience **spaghettification**. Because gravity is so much stronger at your feet than at your head, your body would be stretched into a long, thin strand of atoms. 

However, if you fell into a **supermassive** black hole, the "stretch" is much gentler; you might actually cross the event horizon alive, though you’d still be trapped forever!
