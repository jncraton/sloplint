A **black hole** is one of the most extreme and fascinating objects in the universe—an area in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape.

### 🌀 What creates a black hole?

Most black holes form when a very massive star collapses in on itself at the end of its life. This collapse can happen after a powerful explosion called a **supernova**.

---

### 🌌 Key parts of a black hole

* **Event horizon**: The “point of no return.” Once something crosses this boundary, it can’t escape.
* **Singularity**: The center, where all the mass is crushed into an incredibly tiny space.
* **Accretion disk**: A glowing ring of gas and dust spiraling into the black hole.

---

### ⚡ Why are they so strange?

Black holes warp space and time. Their behavior is explained by **general relativity**, developed by Albert Einstein. Near a black hole:

* Time slows down
* Light bends
* Objects stretch (a process nicknamed “spaghettification”)

---

### 🔭 Can we see black holes?

Not directly—but we can detect them by:

* Watching how nearby stars move
* Observing X-rays from hot material falling in
* Capturing images like the famous one from the Event Horizon Telescope

---

### 🧠 Fun fact

At the center of our galaxy, the **Sagittarius A*** is a supermassive black hole about 4 million times the mass of the Sun.
